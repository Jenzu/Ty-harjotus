
Counter-Strike

Counter-Strike is a first-person shooter video game developed by
Valve Corporation.
It was initially developed and released as a Half-Life modification by
Minh "Gooseman" Le and Jess "Cliffe" Cliffe in 1999.
Counter-Strike is a first-person shooter game in which players
join either the terrorist team,
the counter-terrorist team, or become spectators.
Each team attempts to complete their mission objective
and/or eliminate the opposing team.
Each round starts with the two teams spawning simultaneously.
